Who needs to file.

Filing Requirements

U.S. citizens and resident aliens living abroad must still file U.S. tax and report worldwide income. Depending on your income, you might also need to file additional forms, like a Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (Form 2555) or the Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116).

Foreign Earned Income Exclusion

You can exclude a certain amount of your foreign earned income ($120,000 for 2023) from U.S. taxes if you meet specific criteria, such as passing the bona fide resident or physical presence tests.

Foreign Tax Credit

If you pay taxes to a foreign country, you might be eligible for a credit that can reduce your U.S. tax liability.

Deadlines and Extensions

Although the U.S. tax filing deadline is April 15th, expats receives an automatic extension to June 15th, with a possible further extension to October 15th if requested.

Special Considerations

Expats may also need to consider other forms like the FBAR (for foreign bank accounts) and FATCA, which require reporting of foreign financial assests.

U.S. expat tax returns filing deadlines

Don't miss the Regular Deadline: U.S. Expat Tax Preparation needs to start and end by April 15th, the same as domestic filers. Avail the Automatic Extension: Expat gets an automatic two-month extension to June 15th. Additional Extension: You can request an additional extension to October 15th by filing Form 4868. Special Circumstances: In some cases, you may qualify for a further extension to December 15th by sending a letter to IRS.
