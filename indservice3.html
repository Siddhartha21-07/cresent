ITIN Assistance

Simplify your Taxes with Expert ITIN Support

>

Streamlined guidance, expert help

ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) assistance helps individuals who are not eligible for a Social Security Number but need to fulfil U.S tax obligations. This service includes guiding applicants through the process of obtaining an ITIN, ensuring compliance with IRS requirements, and addressing any issues related to tax filing for non-resident aliens or foreign nationals.

ITIN Application Process

Complete Form W-7: Fill out IRS Form W-7, Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. Prepare Documentation: Gather required documents, such as a valid passport or other identification. Submit Application: Send the completed Form W-7 and supporting documents to IRS, either by mail or through an agent. Receive ITIN: Wait for the IRS to process your application and issue your ITIN, typically within 7-10 weeks.

Eligibility Criteria

Non-Resident Aliens

Individuals needing to file U.S. tax return without an SSN.

Foreign Nationals

Those residing abroad with U.S. tax obligations.

Dependents

Foreign dependents or spouses on a U.S. tax return.

ITIN number renewal and expiry

Renewal

ITINs must be renewed if they are expired or about to expire. Submit Form W-7 with updated information and supporting documents before the ITIN's expiration date to ensure continued validity.

Expiry

ITINs that are not used on a federal tax return for three consecutive years will expire. To reinstate an expired ITIN, you must reapply using Form W-7 with appropriate documentation.
